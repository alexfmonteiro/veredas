## Executive Summary

Seven market niches are ranked by composite attractiveness score
(Market Size x Ease of Entry x Willingness to Pay). The top niches are
LATAM-focused asset managers, fintech data API (B2B2C), and corporate
FP&A teams. A phased GTM strategy moves from free-tier validation to
B2B enterprise to LATAM expansion over 36 months.

## 2. Niche Deep Dives

### Niche #1: LATAM-Focused Asset Managers & Hedge Funds (Score: 80)

**Target buyer**: Portfolio managers, CIOs, macro strategists at funds
investing in LATAM equities, fixed income, or FX.

**Pain point**: Pay $24K/seat for Bloomberg but only use ~5% of features
(macro data). Junior analysts spend hours downloading CSV from BCB/IBGE.
No AI-powered "what changed and why" briefings.

**Buyer universe**: ~200 LATAM-focused funds in Sao Paulo, ~150 in NYC,
~50 in London. Concentrated and reachable.

**Pricing**:
- Team plan: $199-999/month (5-10 seats)
- Enterprise: $2K-5K/month (unlimited seats, API, custom dashboards)

**ROI story**: "Replace $24K Bloomberg seat for macro-only analysts with
$300/month tool. Save $23K/analyst/year."

**GTM approach**:
1. Direct outreach to top 50 LATAM funds in Sao Paulo and NYC
2. Free 3-month Enterprise trial
3. CFA Society Brazil events, ANBIMA conferences
4. Referral from existing users (tight community)

**Conversion triggers**: Quality of AI insights, speed of data access,
anomaly alerts that catch something Bloomberg missed.

---

### Niche #2: Fintech Data API -- B2B2C (Score: 60)

**Target buyer**: CTO/CPO at Brazilian fintechs (digital banks, investment
apps, wealthtechs, lending platforms).

**Pain point**: Need economic context in their app (e.g., "SELIC changed,
here's what it means for your savings") but building data pipelines is not
core competency. Current options: scrape government sites or pay Bloomberg
data fees.

**Buyer universe**: 1,200+ fintechs in Brazil. Key targets: NuBank (90M+
users), Inter (30M+), XP (4M+), BTG Digital, Rico, Clear.

**Pricing**:
- API tier: $500-5K/month based on call volume
- White-label dashboard: $2K-15K/month
- Embeddable widget: $1K-5K/month

**GTM approach**:
1. Developer marketing: API docs, Python/JS SDKs, blog posts
2. Partner with ABFintechs (fintech association)
3. Sponsor fintech events in Brazil
4. Free API tier (100 calls/day) for developer adoption
5. Marketplace listings (RapidAPI, AWS Data Exchange)

**Why high leverage**: Each fintech partnership provides distribution to
millions of end users. 3 contracts at $5K/month = $180K ARR + brand
exposure to 100M+ users.

### Niche #3: Corporate FP&A / Treasury Teams (Score: 60)

**Target buyer**: CFO, Head of FP&A, Treasury manager at mid-large
Brazilian corporations (revenue >R$100M).

**Pain point**: Macro assumptions in financial models (SELIC forecasts,
inflation, FX) are manually updated from government websites. No automated
alerts when rates change. Budget cycles require macro scenario analysis.

**Buyer universe**: ~5,000 mid-large companies in Brazil. Every company
with a Treasury or FP&A function.

**Pricing**:
- Pro: $199-499/month per company
- Enterprise (custom dashboards + API): $1K-5K/month

**GTM approach**:
1. Content marketing: weekly macro economic reports (AI-generated)
2. LinkedIn campaigns targeting CFOs and FP&A managers
3. Partnerships with consulting firms (Deloitte, EY, KPMG Brazil)
4. Integration with Excel/Google Sheets (API + template)
5. SEO: "Brazil SELIC rate today", "IPCA inflation forecast"

**Stickiness**: Once integrated into planning workflows and models, very
high switching costs. Finance teams don't change data sources lightly.

### Niche #4: Economic Journalism / Media (Score: 45)

**Target buyer**: Economic editors, data journalists at Valor Economico,
Folha, Estadao, InfoMoney, Bloomberg Linea.

**Pain point**: Manually pulling data for charts, slow publication cycles,
no automated anomaly alerts.

**Pricing**:
- Free for journalists (PR value)
- Newsroom license: $99-299/month
- Embeddable widget license: $500-2K/month

**GTM approach**:
1. Free accounts for economic journalists
2. "Powered by BR Economic Pulse" branded charts
3. Press releases using platform's anomaly detection

**Strategic value**: Low revenue but high brand exposure. Every chart
published with "Powered by" branding is free marketing.

---

### Niche #5: Multi-Country LATAM Suite (Score: 32)

**Target buyer**: International asset managers, multilateral organizations
(IDB, World Bank), LATAM consulting firms.

**Pain point**: No single platform provides clean, AI-enhanced macro data
across all LATAM economies.

**Pricing**:
- Per-country data feed: $500-2K/month
- Full LATAM bundle: $3K-10K/month

**GTM approach**:
1. Start with Mexico (2nd largest LATAM economy, excellent APIs)
2. Add Chile (3rd priority, strong fintech ecosystem)
3. Package as "LATAM Economic Intelligence Suite"
4. Target multilateral orgs and global macro funds

**Why lower score**: Requires significant engineering per country ($15-30K
each). High potential but higher investment threshold.

---

### Niche #6: Academic / Research Institutions (Score: 30)

**Target buyer**: Economics professors, PhD students at FGV, USP,
UNICAMP, PUC, INSPER.

**Pricing**:
- Free tier (already implemented: 10 queries/day)
- Academic license: $49-99/month
- Institutional: $199-499/month

**Strategic value**: Low revenue but builds brand awareness and pipeline
of future professionals. Every economics student who learns on the
platform becomes a future enterprise buyer.

---

### Niche #7: RegTech / Compliance (Score: 30)

**Target buyer**: Compliance officers at banks, insurance companies,
pension funds.

**Pain point**: Regulatory reporting requires macro data with audit trails.

**Pricing**: $1K-10K/month (compliance premium).

**Why lower score**: High WTP but requires compliance certifications (SOC 2),
audit capabilities, and regulatory knowledge the product doesn't yet have.
Defer to Phase 3.

## 3. Go-to-Market Strategy

### Phase 1: Foundation (Months 0-6)

**Objective**: Validate PMF with paying customers. Target: 50-150 Pro
subscribers, 2-3 Enterprise pilots.

| Action | Channel | Cost | Expected Result |
|---|---|---|---|
| Launch public free tier | Product | $0 | 2,000-5,000 signups |
| Weekly "Brazil Economic Pulse" newsletter | Content | $0 (AI-generated) | 1,000+ subscribers |
| SEO landing pages per series | Content | $0 | Organic traffic |
| API documentation + SDKs | Developer | 2 weeks eng | Developer adoption |
| Direct outreach to 50 funds | Sales | 1 person, 3 months | 2-3 Enterprise pilots |
| Product Hunt / Hacker News launch | Community | $0 | Brand awareness |

**Key metric**: Free-to-paid conversion rate (target: 2-5%).

### Phase 2: Growth (Months 6-18)

**Objective**: Scale to $1M+ ARR. Establish B2B2C channel.

| Action | Channel | Cost | Expected Result |
|---|---|---|---|
| Add 10-15 more Brazilian series | Product | 2-3 weeks eng | Deeper coverage |
| Add Mexico as 2nd country | Product | 4-6 weeks eng | 2x SAM |
| Sign 3-5 fintech white-label deals | B2B2C | 1 sales rep | $180-900K ARR |
| Build embeddable chart widget | Product | 2 weeks eng | White-label enabler |
| SOC 2 Type I certification | Trust | $20-40K | Enterprise requirement |
| Open-source pipeline framework | Community | $0 | Developer trust |

### Phase 3: Scale (Months 18-36)

**Objective**: $5M+ ARR. Full LATAM coverage. Dark factory at scale.

| Action | Channel | Cost | Expected Result |
|---|---|---|---|
| Add Argentina, Colombia, Chile, Peru | Product | 8-12 weeks eng | 6-country coverage |
| Enterprise platform (SSO, RBAC, audit) | Product | 6-8 weeks eng | Enterprise-ready |
| Proprietary derived metrics | Product | Ongoing | Data moat |
| Alternative data sources | Partnerships | Variable | Differentiation |
| Series A fundraise | Capital | N/A | $5-10M at $50-80M valuation |

## Key Takeaway

Start with LATAM asset managers (highest WTP, concentrated buyers, clear
ROI story). Simultaneously build the fintech API channel for B2B2C
leverage. Use free tier + content marketing as the acquisition funnel.
Each niche reinforces the others: media exposure drives signups, academic
adoption builds future enterprise buyers, fintech partnerships provide
distribution at scale.
